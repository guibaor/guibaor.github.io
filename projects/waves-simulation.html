Waves Simulation

Interactive simulation of directional, radial and Gerstner waves using a height map.

The possibility of switching between the different wave type in real time, as well as changing all their parameters and the direction in which they move, has been implemented via keyboard mapping.

This simulation also has a graphical interface with all the information regarding the waves that are being painted and all the different actions that can be performed from the keyboard (alter parameters, paint the nodes of the height map, apply/unapply the texture, etc).


Implementation of a scenario where 6 different Gerstner waves (with different directions, amplitudes, lengths and speeds) are used to simulate the movement of ocean water.


Implementation of a scenario where radio waves are used to simulate the effect of rain on a puddle of water.

This effect has been achieved by modifying the model of radio waves, adding an attenuation of the amplitude proportional to the distance and time in which the wave is created, and reducing the area where the waves appear.

To achieve a more realistic effect, the value of the radius of the waves has also been modified regarding to time.
