Mass-spring systems - Cloth


Simulation of a cloth colliding with a sphere, based on the mass-spring model.

Through the keyboard, the user can choose different layouts for the springs that build up the cloth: perpendicular (structural), diagonal (shear), curved (bend) and the different combinations between them.

Different views of the cloth can be chosen through the keyboard (transparent/color and grid/no grid) Initially, the colth is anchored by the four corners and pressing space these anchor points disappear and the cloths starts falling due to gravity, until colliding with the sphere and/or the plane.


Interactive simulation of a deformable 2D mesh, based on the mass-spring model.

Through the keyboard, the user can choose different layouts for the springs that build up the mesh: perpendicular (structural), diagonal (shear), curved (bend) and the different combinations between them.

Changing between the spring layouts, the differences in elasticity and resistance between the different structures become visible. Ball-net collision has also been implemented, putting springs where the ball and net collide to make the ball bounce. On the other hand, the net can be made unbreakable to view the ball collision behavior of each spring structure.


Simple and interactive hair simulation, based on the mass-spring model in one dimension.

By clicking on any node and moving the mouse, the position of the clicked node will be changed, causing all the springs that make up that "hair" to move in a similar way to sharing a hair or a rope.

It is possible to modify the number of hairs as well as their length and the position where they appear.
